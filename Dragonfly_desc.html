Dragonfly

HAs members of the Odonata order, dragonflies are predatory insects. Their bodies grow longer, their wings are transparent, and their vision is superb. In order to seek for smaller insects, dragonflies are frequently found close to freshwater areas. They have three life stages: egg, nymph, and adult; their metamorphosis is partial. While adults catch their food midair, nymphs are aquatic and feed on tiny invertebrates. Because they control insect populations and act as environmental health indicators, dragonflies are essential to freshwater ecosystems.
