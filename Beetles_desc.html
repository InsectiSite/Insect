Beetle

As a diverse group of insects, beetles make up almost 40% of all known insect species and are members of the Coleoptera order. The stiffened forewings, or elytra, that distinguish them. The world's beetles are diverse in their sizes, forms, colors, and ways of living. They live in a multitude of settings. They may eat other insects, manure, fungus, plants, and carrion. Through the stages of egg, larva, pupa, and adult, beetles go through complete metamorphosis. As scavengers, pollinators, predators, and decomposers, they are essential to ecosystems.
